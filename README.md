## What is Tor and Why Explore Its Websites?

The Tor network, short for The Onion Router, is a free overlay network designed for anonymous communication. Launched in 2002, it routes your internet traffic through multiple volunteer-operated relays to hide your IP address and browsing activity. In 2026, Tor remains essential for privacy advocates, journalists, and those in restrictive regimes.

Tor's .onion sites—hidden services accessible only via the Tor browser—offer unparalleled anonymity. From secure email to uncensored news, these websites bypass censorship. However, the dark web's reputation stems from unregulated content, so caution is key.
